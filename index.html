Are you interested in a career in health care? If so, which field and why?:

  • One of the most beautiful uses of design and technology I have ever seen is the CT Pirate Island Adventure. Long story short, children were so nervous that they had trouble staying still when given CT scans. This would interfere with the machine's ability to do what it's supposed to, and the children would have to be sedated. GE invented a series of CT scanners where the process would become a game for the child to 'play.' ("Please don't move when you get under the tunnel, or the other pirates will hear you!" Kid stays still and the scan is taken successfully. Sometimes kids even ask to take the CT again!) Beautiful display of how art and technology can transform scary exams into fun activities.

Why are you interested in being a part of this program and what do you hope to get out of this program?

There are some experiences at UF that go beyond the call of action. These groups find a way for us to change a life. They open us to levels of vulnerability and human connection, levels that go so far past our comfort zones that the zones become invisible to us, even with telescopes. Experiences that challenge us and shape us and mold us and break us down and build us up. Experiences that make us laugh after they make us cry. That make us happy to stay overtime. That can never be encaptured by a line on a resume or a single story.

Footprints to me is one of these experiences.

I don't know what I expect to get out of Footprints. I'd hate to sketch out my rough ideas with letters and words because they would just sound silly. It's hardly relevant, though. I do not want to do Footprints because of what I would get out of it. That seems hardly fair to the children, who need it miles more than I do. I hope to give to this program much more than I can carry away with these hands.
